Energy Usage Prediction

Millions of dollars are at stake for Texas energy retailers. Due to the deregulated energy market, being able to accurately forecast future energy usage can have a large impact on a retailer's profit.

For Baylor's Spring 2016 Capstone Design project, my team and I are responsible for creating a system that predicts future energy load. This project is ongoing, so this page will likely change.

Architecture

We decided to build microservices for this project. One of my group mates had experience using them in production, and I'd been wanting to get some hands-on experience with them.

Because I enjoy data problems and machine learning, I called dibs on designing/implementing the service which did the predictions. To design the service, I worked with my primary team member who would be consuming the service. Designing the interface in this way was a good choice because we were able to talk through implementation issues and usage issues.

Process

After we were done writing our notes on a whiteboard, I wrote tests (using pytest) for each bit of functionality we came up with. Having something to shoot for, I was then able to implement the service. This test-driven development process was useful for me, because I could see tests lighting up green as I got things working. I also like the idea of having tests because they can ensure the interface is being followed.
